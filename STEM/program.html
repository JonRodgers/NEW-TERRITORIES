Program Structure

The 100-class curriculum is organized into 10 thematic sections, each running for 2 weeks (15 classes per week):

  • ✓ Week 1-2: Transportation & Mobility (Classes 1-15)
  • ✓ Week 3-4: Architecture & Structures (Classes 16-30)
  • ✓ Week 5-6: Energy & Power (Classes 31-45)
  • ✓ Week 7-8: Water & Sanitation (Classes 46-60)
  • ✓ Week 9-10: Materials & Manufacturing (Classes 61-75)
  • ✓ Week 11-12: Communication & Information (Classes 76-90)
  • ✓ Week 13-14: Medicine & Health (Classes 91-100)
  • ✓ Week 15-16: Environmental & Sustainability (Classes 101-115)
  • ✓ Week 17-18: Space & Exploration (Classes 116-130)
  • ✓ Week 19-20: Future Technologies & Innovation (Classes 131-145)

Learning Outcomes

By completing this 100-class curriculum, students will:

  • Understand the historical evolution of major technologies and innovations
  • Develop hands-on engineering and design skills across multiple disciplines
  • Learn to think critically about real-world problems and solutions
  • Gain exposure to careers in STEM fields and innovation
  • Build confidence in tackling complex, interdisciplinary challenges
  • Develop creativity and imagination through project-based learning
  • Understand the intersection of science, technology, engineering, art, and mathematics
  • Learn from pioneers and innovators who shaped our world
  • Apply knowledge to design solutions for real-world problems
  • Develop teamwork and communication skills through collaborative projects
